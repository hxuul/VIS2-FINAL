Overview

The DSS Benefit and Payment Recipient Demographics dataset provides detailed insights into the distribution of social service payments across Australia. Compiled and updated quarterly by the Department of Social Services (DSS), this dataset covers various payment types, including the Age Pension, Carer Payment, JobSeeker Payment, Youth Allowance, and many more.

The data is categorized by key demographics such as age, gender, and Indigenous/non-Indigenous status, offering a granular view of how social service benefits are distributed across different population segments. Additionally, the dataset includes geographic breakdowns by state/territory, postcode, Local Government Areas (LGAs), and SA2 regions (small statistical areas).

description

This visualization provides a global overview of the distribution of social service payments per 10,000 population. While the data appears to show that Australia has the highest payment count, it's essential to note that the figures are normalized per capita. In reality, countries with larger populations, such as China, have higher total payment counts, but this visualization adjusts the numbers to account for population size, making the payments comparable across different regions.

Additionally, by selecting a country on the map, you can view detailed information about that country's specific payment data. This interactive feature allows for deeper exploration of each regionâ€™s data, making it easier to compare social service distributions globally.

The bottom visualization showcases the **Breakdown of Social Service Payments by Benefit Type** for a selected country, (default to be Total) focusing on total counts across various payment categories. Pensioner Concession and Age Pension are the most prominent benefit types Overall, accounting for a significant portion of social service payments.

1. Duration of Payments by Payment Type:
The stacked bar chart on the left shows the total count of individuals receiving different types of payments categorized by the duration they have been receiving these payments. The categories of duration include: Under 1 year, 1-<2 years, 2-<5 years, 5-<10 years, and 10 years+.
The Age Pension has the highest total count across all durations, with a large portion of recipients in the 10+ years category.
JobSeeker Payment, Disability Pension, and Carer Payment also show significant distributions, with most individuals in these categories receiving payments for 1 to 2 years or more.

2. Rate of Earnings by Payment Type:
The bar chart above provides insights into the rate of earnings (or lack thereof) for recipients of various payment types.
Comparing both, it is clear that some payment types (like Age Pension and Carer Payment) are characterized by long-term recipients with little or no earnings, while other payments are more likely to be short-term with a mix of earners and non-earners.
